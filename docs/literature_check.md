# Literature check: does a replication of this paper already exist?

**Question:** Has anyone published a replication or modern-estimator reassessment of the crime result in Cáceres-Delpiano and Giolito (2012), "The Impact of Unilateral Divorce on Crime" (*Journal of Labor Economics* 30(1))?

**Conclusion:** No. As of July 2026, no published replication of the divorce–crime result exists, and no reassessment using post-2020 heterogeneity-robust difference-in-differences estimators. This project appears to be the first public replication of the crime result specifically.

**Search date:** July 1, 2026

## Sources searched

### 1. RePEc / CitEc citation index
- 29 citing works listed: https://ideas.repec.org/r/iza/izadps/dp3380.html
- All 29 reviewed. They fall into three groups: papers using the original as a background reference while studying adjacent outcomes (divorce law and house prices, intimate partner violence, children's long-term outcomes, marital bargaining); crime papers borrowing the design for a different treatment (closest: Ciacci and Sansone on sodomy law repeals); and Shen (2018), "Marriage, Divorce and Sorting: A Reassessment of Unilateral Divorce Laws," which despite the title is about marriage-market sorting, not crime.
- None is a replication or reassessment of the crime result.

### 2. Google Scholar citation set
- 95 citing works: https://scholar.google.com/scholar?cites=11064034127391747694
- Searched within the citing works for: "replication," "revisiting," "reassessment," "Callaway," "Chaisemartin," "heterogeneous treatment."
- Only matches: the de Chaisemartin and d'Haultfœuille TWFE methodology survey, which cites the paper as one staggered-adoption example among dozens but re-runs Wolfers' (2006) divorce-rate application, not the crime result; and generic methodology papers about replication as a practice.

### 3. GitHub
- Zero repositories match "Caceres-Delpiano divorce crime" or "unilateral divorce crime replication."
- 18 scattered code hits mention the paper; none is a dedicated replication.

## Adjacent work (context for positioning)

- The unilateral divorce setting is a canonical testing ground for modern DiD estimators, but the applications re-run are Wolfers (2006) on divorce rates and Stevenson and Wolfers (2006) on female suicide and domestic violence, not the crime outcome.
- Stevenson and Wolfers (2006) find the same reforms reduced female suicide and domestic violence, so the literature's welfare findings are mixed across outcomes.

## Limitations of this search

- Google Scholar's within-citations search only catches papers whose title or abstract uses the searched keywords; a replication describing itself differently could slip through.
- The 95 citing works were filtered by keyword, not read individually.
- Non-English publications and unindexed working papers are not well covered by these sources.
